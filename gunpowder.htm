The Gunpowder Plot | Remember Remember the 5th of November

Performing on Wurundjeri land, Alchemy explores the Gunpowder Plot, its origins and legacy in the intriguing spaces of a Gold Rush gunpowder store on the banks of the Maribyrnong River. The thirteen voices of Alchemy are joined by narrator and pianist as we discover divisions, secrecy, conflict and prayers for peace, from the Renaissance to the late-20th century.

      Jack's Magazine | Working Heritage

      Alchemy is hosted at Jack’s Magazine, a sprawling series of indoor and outdoor spaces, hidden along the banks of the Maribyrnong River. The imposing bluestone walls, magazines and earth mounds of Jack’s Magazine have connections to turning points in Victoria’s history – from the Gold Rush to World Wars. Working Heritage has been managing Jack’s Magazine since June 2015

      Your Comfort

      Our performance venue is a large, outdoor site with grassy paths. We will be moving throughout the venue as the concert unfolds. Although we’re performing indoors, we recommend you wear warm clothing and sturdy shoes to ensure your comfort and ease of travel around the venue. Parking is plentiful but may involve up to 5-10 minutes’ walk to the venue. Parking close to the venue will be reserved for audience members with accessibility requirements. Please contact us at alchemyconsort@gmail.com if you have accessibility requirements so we can plan for your needs. 

       

      HOW TO GET THERE

      Google Maps and other services do not provide accurate directions to Jack’s Magazine. Please find your way to the Pipemaker’s Park car park in Maribyrnong or use the following link (https://goo.gl/maps/4LbtwZv623MF1JHX7) and our volunteers will direct you to the venue. Vehicle access and parking is through the adjacent park. Nearest public transport access is via tram (route 82, stop 55) or bus (route 406, Birdwood St/Gordon St stop). 

       

      Please arrive at 6.30pm for a 7.00pm start. This will allow for parking, seating, and a short walk around Jack’s Magazine.

       

       

      Alchemy recognises the ongoing connection of the Wurundjeri people of the Kulin nation to the lands on which we gather to rehearse and perform for this concert. We acknowledge their custodianship of land, waters and culture and the role of music, dance, ceremony and storytelling in these relationships. We pay our respects to elders past, present and emerging and acknowledge that sovereignty was never ceded. Always was, always will be.
